Training

Even if your diet is the most important, if you don’t train correctly according to your goal, you will not get the result expected. For people who have never been to the gym and want to start working out, I would advise you to take a personal trainer that will teach you all the exercises execution, it might cost you money, but you don’t have to keep training with him/her in long term. To get started you must find someone experienced who can show you the moves, otherwise, you might get injured or waste your time.

For now, I will be providing advice only for the two people's most common goals, which are weight loss/shredding, and muscle gain/bulking.

Wheight loss / Shredding

If you are training to lose fat or to shred, the first thing you should know is you cannot target a specific part of your body to lose weight, the weight loss is global, and the best and only way to lose fat wherever it is on your body is to burn calories.

The best way to burn calories is to do a lot of cardio (running, skipping rope, cycling, and so on). So, if your gym session also includes weightlifting, know that you will need to spend more time on cardio than weightlifting.

Besides the cardio, when you workout to shred I will advise you to favorise a dynamic workout and to do longer repetitions and more sets, for example, if you normally do 3 sets of 10 repetitions, change it to 4 sets of 15 repetitions with a lighter weight.


The clue is to burn as many calories as you can, the more calories you burn the more fat you lose.

Bonus: Regardless of if you are shredding or bulking, you must organise your work, g with the gym with a plan, don’t start think about what you’re going to train once you got to the gym.

Don’t try to do a full body workout every day, you can focus your workout on 2 part of the body per day, so you work better on those parts, and you let them recover while you are training other parts which create like a cycle where you can exercise and recover effectively.

Gaining muscle / Bulking

If you are training to build muscles your workout is going to be different, instead of doing more cardiovascular, here you can do a bit of cardio to warm up and avoid injuries, but everything will happen in the weightlifting part of the workout.

Instead of doing more repetitions with light weights here You will he to do fewer repetitions with heavier weights, for example, if you normally do 4 sets of 10 reps now you must do 8 or 6 reps each set with heavier weight.

The most common error people do while training for bulking is to go too heavier and to start paying less attention to the exercise execution. That’s very wrong and dangerous, always prioritize the execution over the weight, try to do clean sets, and lift heavier at the same time.


Concerning the body types, Endomorphs and Mesomorph do not really have issues while bulking but ectomorphs can struggle to get started, it will require a lot of discipline and determination.
